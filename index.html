The Silent Land Grab
of Muktainagar

How a missing entry in city survey records is enabling large-scale encroachment on outsider-owned plots in Punarvasan Tappa 2 — and why the system refuses to act.

Maharashtra  ·  Concerned Citizens  ·  2025

In the quiet resettlement colony of Muktainagar Punarvasan Tappa 2, a slow and methodical land grab is underway. Plots purchased legally by citizens from outside the region are being encroached upon, boundaries are being erased, and those responsible face no accountability. At the heart of this crisis lies a bureaucratic loophole that has been quietly exploited — the absence of these plots from the official City Survey records.

Punarvasan Tappa 2 is a government-designated resettlement zone. People from across Maharashtra purchased plots here in good faith, trusting that a government-backed scheme would guarantee their rights. But for many of those buyers — particularly those who do not live locally — that trust has proven dangerously misplaced.

"When your plot is not in the City Survey, it is as if it does not exist on paper — and what doesn't exist on paper can be taken."

The Loophole That Started It All

The plots in Punarvasan Tappa 2 have not been included in the official City Survey — the government's primary mechanism for recording, measuring, and protecting urban land ownership. Without this inclusion, a legally valid official survey (mojni) of these plots cannot be conducted through proper channels. No survey means no verified boundary. No verified boundary means no protection.

This is not a minor administrative oversight. It is the structural weakness that has allowed a coordinated pattern of encroachment to flourish. Local actors, fully aware of this gap, have moved quickly to exploit it — occupying land that does not belong to them, carrying out unauthorised and fake measurements, and quietly expanding their hold over plots whose owners live far away and rarely visit.

Outsiders Targeted, Locals Protected

The pattern is unmistakable. Plot owners who are not residents of Muktainagar — those who bought land as an investment or for future settlement — are the primary targets. Their absence from the locality makes them vulnerable. By the time they discover what has happened, encroachment is already deeply entrenched.

What Is Happening on the Ground

  • Plots of non-local owners being physically occupied by local individuals
  • Fake and unauthorised land measurements being conducted to falsify boundaries
  • Local complaints going unheard or actively suppressed
  • Local administrative bodies appearing to support the encroachers
  • Formal complaints filed but never acted upon or forwarded to higher authorities

What makes this particularly alarming is the reported complicity of local authorities. Multiple complaints have been filed by affected plot owners, yet no action has followed. Complaints appear to be buried at the local level, never reaching the desks of officers who could actually intervene. When institutions designed to protect citizens instead shield the accused, ordinary people have nowhere left to turn.

The Bigger Warning for Maharashtra

This is not merely a local dispute. It is a warning signal for the entire state. If government-allotted plots under official resettlement schemes can be encroached upon without consequence — if legal ownership means nothing without the backing of survey records, and if local authorities can suppress complaints with impunity — then no one in Maharashtra can feel secure buying land outside their home district.

The message being sent, whether intentionally or not, is deeply troubling: outsiders who invest in Maharashtra's government schemes do so at their own risk. That is a message no state with aspirations of development and rule of law can afford to send.

If legal ownership means nothing when survey records are missing, then who will dare invest in Maharashtra's land again?

What Needs to Happen Now

Immediate Demands

  1. Urgent independent inquiry into all disputed plots in Muktainagar Punarvasan Tappa 2, conducted by senior officials free from local influence.
  2. Official government survey of all plots in Punarvasan Tappa 2 to establish verified boundaries and deliver formal reports to rightful owners.
  3. Immediate inclusion of Punarvasan Tappa 2 in the official City Survey (Shahar Bhoomapan) records — this is the single most effective long-term solution.
  4. Criminal action against those conducting fake surveys and encroaching on private land under the Maharashtra Land Revenue Code and the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita.
  5. Independent inquiry into local officials who failed to act on or deliberately suppressed legitimate complaints.

A Policy Fix That Could Prevent This Everywhere

Beyond Muktainagar, there is a systemic fix that Maharashtra urgently needs. Every plot allotted under any government resettlement or rehabilitation scheme must be automatically included in the City Survey at the time of allotment. This single policy change would close the loophole being exploited in Muktainagar and prevent similar crises from emerging elsewhere in the state.

When the government grants land to a citizen under an official scheme, it carries an implicit promise — that the citizen's ownership will be recorded, protected, and enforceable. Without City Survey inclusion, that promise is hollow. Maharashtra's government has both the authority and the responsibility to make that promise real.

The people of Punarvasan Tappa 2 are not asking for favours. They are asking for what the law already guarantees them: the right to own what they have legally purchased, the right to have their land measured and recorded, and the right to have their complaints heard. It is time the authorities listened.
